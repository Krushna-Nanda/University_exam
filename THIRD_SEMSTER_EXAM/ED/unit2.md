## **Discuss the role of business incubators in supporting startups. (6 Marks)**

Business incubators are organizations that help new startups grow by providing support, resources, and guidance in the early stage of business. They act like a **‚Äúsupport system‚Äù** for entrepreneurs who have an idea but do not know how to start.

### **Roles of Business Incubators:**

---

#### **1Ô∏è‚É£ Provide Workspace and Infrastructure**

Incubators give office space, internet, conference rooms, computers, and other basic facilities at low or no cost.
**Example:** A startup building a mobile app works in an incubator instead of renting an expensive office.

---

#### **2Ô∏è‚É£ Mentorship and Expert Guidance**

Experienced business professionals and industry experts guide startups in planning, finance, marketing, and technology.
**Example:** A student startup receives guidance from IIT professors on improving product design.

---

#### **3Ô∏è‚É£ Access to Funding and Investors**

Incubators connect startups with banks, angel investors, and venture capitalists for funding.

**Example:** T-Hub in Hyderabad connects startups with investors for financial support.

---

#### **4Ô∏è‚É£ Training and Skill Development**

Incubators conduct workshops, seminars, and training on accounting, digital marketing, teamwork, leadership, and communication.

**Example:** A founder learns how to pitch ideas to investors through pitch-training sessions.

---

#### **5Ô∏è‚É£ Networking Opportunities**

Startups meet other entrepreneurs, industry leaders, and government officials.
Networking helps in collaboration, partnerships, and business growth.

**Example:** A food startup collaborates with a delivery company after meeting them in the incubator.

---

#### **6Ô∏è‚É£ Reduced Risk and Increased Success**

With support, startups make fewer mistakes and have a better chance of survival and success.

**Example:** A hardware startup avoids production failure because mentors guide them before mass manufacturing.
