# **Classify Entrepreneurs Based on Size of Enterprise (6 Marks)**

Entrepreneurs can be classified on the basis of the **size of the business** they establish and manage. The common classifications are **Small Entrepreneurs, Medium Entrepreneurs, and Large Entrepreneurs**. Each type differs in scale of operations, number of employees, level of investment, and market reach.

---

## **1. Small Entrepreneurs**

**Meaning:**
These entrepreneurs run **small-scale enterprises** with **low investment**, limited employees, and local market operations. They usually start with personal savings or small loans.

**Features:**

* Labour-intensive, simple technology
* Limited production capacity
* Close contact with customers
* Local area operations

**Example:**
A person running a small bakery, tailoring shop, mobile repair shop, or stationery store.

---

## **2. Medium Entrepreneurs**

**Meaning:**
These entrepreneurs manage **medium-sized enterprises** with **moderate investment**, a structured workforce, and a wider market than small units. They use semi-automatic or modern technology.

**Features:**

* More employees (20‚Äì100)
* Higher investment and organized structure
* Better quality control and marketing systems
* Serve city or regional markets

**Example:**
A medium-sized textile manufacturing unit or a regional packaged-food company.

---

## **3. Large Entrepreneurs**

**Meaning:**
Large entrepreneurs establish **large-scale industries** with **high investment**, advanced technology, and operations spread across national or international markets. They employ a large workforce.

**Features:**

* High capital investment
* Modern machinery and automation
* Professional management teams
* Nationwide or global market

**Example:**
Founders of large companies like Tata Steel, Reliance Industries, or a large automobile manufacturing plant.

---

# **Conclusion**

Classifying entrepreneurs based on enterprise size helps understand the scale of operations, investment level, and market reach.
Small, medium, and large entrepreneurs each contribute differently to economic development.
