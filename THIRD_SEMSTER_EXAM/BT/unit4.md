# ‚≠ê **Applications of Blockchain in Insurance, Healthcare, Cloud Computing and Artificial Intelligence**

(6 Marks)

Blockchain technology provides decentralization, transparency, immutability, and secure data sharing. Because of these features, it is widely used in insurance, healthcare, cloud systems, and artificial intelligence.

---

## **1. Blockchain in Insurance**

### ‚úî Fraud Reduction

* Insurance fraud is common, such as fake claims or duplicate claims.
* With blockchain, every claim is recorded in a tamper-proof ledger.
* Example: If a person tries to claim the same accident in two companies, the blockchain record will immediately show the duplicate claim.

### ‚úî Faster Claim Processing

* Smart contracts can automatically verify conditions and release payments.
* Example: In flight-delay insurance, if a flight is delayed by 3 hours, a smart contract automatically pays the customer without paperwork.

### ‚úî Secure Customer Data

* Customer details like policies and claim history cannot be modified or manipulated.

---

## **2. Blockchain in Healthcare**

### ‚úî Secure Medical Records

* Patient records are stored in a secure and immutable way.
* Only authorized doctors can access them.
* Example: If a patient visits another hospital in a different city, the doctor can view verified medical history instantly through the blockchain.

### ‚úî Preventing Fake Medicines

* Blockchain tracks the entire supply chain of medicines from manufacturer to pharmacy.
* Example: If a fake medicine enters the market, the absence of its blockchain record exposes it immediately.

### ‚úî Medical Insurance Coordination

* Hospital, insurance company, and patient share the same data without disputes.

---

## **3. Blockchain in Cloud Computing**

### ‚úî Decentralized Storage

* Instead of storing files in a single server (which can fail or be hacked), blockchain-based cloud stores data across multiple nodes.
* Example: Platforms like IPFS store files in a distributed manner, making them more secure and available.

### ‚úî Data Integrity

* Any file stored in cloud can be verified using blockchain hashes.
* Example: If someone tries to modify a stored document, its hash will change, proving tampering.

### ‚úî Reduced Downtime

* Because data is spread across many nodes, services remain available even if one server fails.

---

## **4. Blockchain in Artificial Intelligence (AI)**

### ‚úî Trusted Data for AI Models

* AI models need large amounts of accurate data. Blockchain ensures the data used for training is genuine and has not been altered.
* Example: A hospital AI model predicting diseases can trust the patient data stored on blockchain.

### ‚úî Data Ownership & Transparency

* Users can control who uses their data for AI training.
* Example: A user can allow an AI company to use their browsing history in exchange for tokens.

### ‚úî Explainable and Audit-Friendly AI

* All steps of an AI decision can be stored on blockchain, allowing audits.
* Example: In loan approval AI, banks can check the blockchain log to understand why a loan was rejected.
